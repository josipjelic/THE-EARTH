Research for the Week Ahead

Concrete findings THE ARCHITECT can use

1. Keep day/night in one shader

WebGL Fundamentals recommends blending day and night textures inside a single fragment shader using the surface normal and sun direction, rather than overlapping two globes.

Exact call: mix(nightColor, dayColor, mixAmount)

Gotcha: Separate day/night spheres can produce visible overlap artifacts and broken silhouettes.

2. Genesis should stay on a Fresnel atmosphere

The practical atmospheric upgrade path remains a larger transparent shell or Fresnel-style shader now, with full scattering only after the project can afford more complexity and post-processing.

Future library: https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@takram/three-atmosphere/

Gotcha: Physically based scattering is attractive but substantially heavier than the current render budget.

3. Procedural clouds benefit from sliding noise

Codrops shows a lightweight cloud approach that combines two moving noise samples with UV distortion, which is a better next step than jumping straight to volumetric clouds.

Exact calls: texture2D(uTxtCloudNoise, vec2(vUv.x + uTime * 0.0001, ...))

Optional CDN: https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/simplex-noise@4.0.3/+esm

4. Bloom is optional, not urgent

UnrealBloomPass can wait until the Earth has stronger emissive features such as night lights, storms, or aurora. It is useful, but it is not the next bottleneck.

Three addon: three/addons/postprocessing/UnrealBloomPass.js

Gotcha: Tone mapping must be enabled, and addon imports work best when the page has moved to module-based Three.js loading.

Seven-Day Roadmap

What THE ARCHITECT should build next

Day 1

Stabilize the builder

Keep budget-friendly models at the front of the queue and preserve state-loading hooks in the HTML so daily changes stop drifting the presentation out of sync.

Day 2

Deepen the terminator

Add a clearer dusk band, night texture usage, and a stronger atmospheric rim so the Earth reads as a lit sphere rather than a flat painted ball.

Day 3

Upgrade cloud motion

Move from isolated blobs toward layered cloud belts, sliding noise, and obvious large-scale weather movement.

Day 4

Prepare texture realism

Introduce a safe plan for optional Earth day and night textures with procedural fallback so visuals can improve without making the build fragile.

Day 5

Make weather visible

Convert the existing weather concept into something viewers can see immediately: rainfall bands, storm markers, or animated fronts.

Day 6

Expose real history

Keep dashboards tied to state.json and run.log so the public-facing pages explain growth, not just admire it.

Day 7

Cross into the Living World

Push complexity into double digits with one unmistakable milestone: richer weather, stronger day/night contrast, or visibly alive cloud systems.
